## Week-1 Validation Experiments

### Experiment 1: Pre-Sale Test

**Objective:** Validate willingness to pay before spending on infrastructure.
**Budget:** $50 USD
**Timeline:** 7 days

**Runbook:**

1. **Day 1, Hour 1 — Create a simple offer document.** Write a 1-page PDF or WhatsApp message describing KoneksAI: what it does, what is included in setup, the monthly cost (7,500 HTG setup / 5,000–10,000 HTG/month), and what the client gets in the first 72 hours. No website needed. A Google Doc link or WhatsApp message is sufficient.

2. **Day 1, Hour 2 — Build your target list of 30 businesses.** Walk or drive through Pétion-Ville, Delmas 33, and Tabarre. Note the WhatsApp numbers of restaurants, pharmacies, boutiques, and salons. Alternatively, search Facebook groups for Port-au-Prince businesses and collect 30 contacts. Record in a spreadsheet: business name, type, WhatsApp number, Facebook page URL.

3. **Day 1, Hour 3 — Craft your pre-sale WhatsApp message.** Write it in Haitian Creole: "Bonjou [Nom], mwen se [Ou] nan KoneksAI. Nou bati sistèm WhatsApp otomatik pou biznis tankou pa ou pou $75 USD konfigirasyon + $50/mwa. Mwen ofri 3 premye kliyan yon demo gratis epi yon mwa gratis. Ou enterese pou aprann plis? M ka voye detay yo." Keep it under 100 words. Do not pitch immediately — ask first.

4. **Day 1, Hour 4 — Send to the first 10 contacts.** Do not send all 30 at once. Test the first batch to see which message version gets replies. Note response rate and response time for each contact in your spreadsheet.

5. **Day 2 — Analyze first batch and adjust message.** If fewer than 2 out of 10 replied, rewrite the opening line. If 3 or more replied, send the same message to the next 10 contacts.

6. **Day 2 — Send second batch of 10 contacts.** Follow up with the first batch who did not reply with a single follow-up: "Mwen jis vle verifye si ou te wè mesaj mwen an. Pa gen pwoblèm si ou pa enterese — jis fè m konnen!"

7. **Day 3 — For anyone who expressed interest, send the offer document.** Offer a live WhatsApp or voice call demo. Show them exactly what the automation would look like for their business specifically (use a mock-up chatflow on your phone). Ask: "Eske ou vle rezève yon espas? Mwen aksepte MonCash oswa lajan kach pou kòmanse."

8. **Day 4–5 — Follow up with all warm leads (anyone who replied positively).** Send one follow-up message: "Mwen toujou gen espas pou 2 kliyan yo. Ou ka kòmanse wikenn sa a." Scarcity is real — you actually have limited setup capacity.

9. **Day 6 — Request pre-payment or deposit.** For any business ready to proceed: ask for 50% deposit via MonCash (3,750 HTG) to confirm the setup slot. Confirm their WhatsApp number, business name, and preferred setup time.

10. **Day 7 — Count results and decide.** Log: how many contacts reached, how many replied, how many took the demo, how many pre-paid. Update your spreadsheet with full data.

**Success Threshold:** 3 pre-orders (deposits received) in 7 days.
**Failure Threshold:** 0 pre-orders in 7 days.

**If success (3+ pre-orders):**
- Proceed to build out your WhatsApp Business API account and automation platform (ManyChat, Respond.io, or WhatsApp Cloud API)
- Schedule setup calls for each client for weeks 2–3
- Allocate $200 of seed capital toward Meta Ads to scale lead generation (Experiment 2)
- Begin building your case studies from these first three clients

**If failure (0 pre-orders):**
- Do not abandon the business — diagnose the specific failure point: Was it the message? The price? The product concept? The target segment?
- Request feedback from the 5 most engaged non-converters: "Eske ou ka di m pou ki sa ou pa t enskri? Mwen vle aprann."
- Test a lower entry price: offer the first client free setup for a 90-day testimonial agreement
- Try a different business segment (switch from restaurants to pharmacies or salons)
- Run Experiment 3 (WhatsApp cold DM) simultaneously before concluding demand is absent

### Experiment 2: Paid Ad Test

**Objective:** Validate Meta Ads as a lead generation channel with measurable CPL.
**Budget:** $100 USD (split: $70 Facebook/Instagram, $30 WhatsApp Click-to-Chat)
**Timeline:** 7 days

**Runbook:**

1. **Day 1, Hour 1 — Set up Meta Business Suite.** Create a Facebook Business Page for KoneksAI if not already done. Upload a profile photo (clean logo) and cover photo (WhatsApp automation screenshot or simple tagline graphic). Write a compelling About section in French and Creole. Set up WhatsApp Business phone number linked to the page.

2. **Day 1, Hour 2 — Set up Meta Ads Manager.** Connect your Business Page to Ads Manager. Add a payment method (international credit/debit card or prepaid card). Set your account timezone to EST (Port-au-Prince). Set your currency to USD for easier budget tracking.

3. **Day 1, Hour 3 — Create your Lead Generation campaign.** Campaign objective: Leads (use Instant Experience Form or Click-to-WhatsApp). Budget: $15/day for 7 days. Audience: Port-au-Prince 15km radius, ages 25–55, interests: business, restaurants, pharmacies, retail. Placement: Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed (not all placements — disable Audience Network).

4. **Day 1, Hour 4 — Build two ad sets.** Ad Set A: Variant 1 (pain-based) targeting cold audience. Ad Set B: Variant 2 (result-based) targeting cold audience. $7.50/day each. Both run simultaneously for the first 3 days to identify the winner.

5. **Day 2 — Monitor early data.** Check: impressions, click-through rate (CTR), cost per click (CPC). At this stage do not optimize — let Facebook's algorithm gather data. Flag any ad that gets disapproved and resubmit with compliance-adjusted copy (avoid "you" direct health/financial claims).

6. **Day 3 — Kill the underperformer.** After 3 days, compare CPL (cost per lead) between Ad Set A and B. Turn off the higher-CPL ad set. Reallocate its remaining budget ($22.50) to the winner ad set.

7. **Day 4 — Add the WhatsApp Click-to-Chat placement.** Launch a separate $30 campaign using Click-to-WhatsApp objective. Use Variant 1 or 2 (whichever is winning). Message opener: "Mwen vle aprann plis sou otomatizasyon WhatsApp pou biznis mwen."

8. **Day 5 — Respond to all incoming leads within 15 minutes.** Every lead who fills out the form or sends a WhatsApp should receive a personal reply within 15 minutes during business hours and an automated reply at all other times (set up this automated reply before the campaign launches). Log each lead: name, business type, contact, date, next follow-up date.

9. **Day 6 — Follow up with all Day 1–5 leads.** Send a single WhatsApp follow-up: "Bonjou [Nom], mwen vle verifye si ou gen kesyon sou KoneksAI. Mwen disponib pou yon apèl rapido oswa demo jodi a."

10. **Day 7 — Calculate results.** Total spend / total leads = CPL. Count: leads generated, demos booked, trials started, clients closed. Document lessons: which ad set won, which audience segment responded best, which business types were most responsive.

**Success Threshold:** CPL below $5 USD, minimum 20 leads generated in 7 days.
**Failure Threshold:** CPL above $15 USD OR fewer than 5 leads in 7 days.

**If success (CPL < $5, 20+ leads):**
- Increase daily budget to $20–25/day for weeks 2–3
- Create Lookalike Audiences based on leads who converted to trials
- Build retargeting audiences from video viewers and form starters
- Begin Variant 3 (authority-based) as a third ad set test
- Set a 90-day goal of 50 leads/week at sub-$5 CPL

**If failure (CPL > $15 or < 5 leads):**
- Pause ads immediately — do not burn remaining budget on a broken funnel
- Diagnose: Was the creative wrong? The audience too broad? The offer unclear? The landing page/WhatsApp opener weak?
- Test one variable at a time: rewrite just the headline and relaunch for 3 days
- Try a narrower audience: only restaurants, only pharmacies (single niche)
- Consider organic content (reels, posts) as a cost-free alternative while you diagnose paid

### Experiment 3: WhatsApp Cold DM Outreach Test

**Objective:** Validate direct outreach as a lead generation channel without ad spend.
**Budget:** $0 cash (time cost: approximately 6 hours over 7 days)
**Timeline:** 7 days

**Runbook:**

1. **Day 1, Hour 1 — Build a list of 50 businesses with WhatsApp numbers.** Sources: Facebook Marketplace, Facebook business pages with phone numbers visible, Google Maps searches for "restoran Pòtoprens," "famasi Pétion-Ville," "boutik Delmas," local Facebook groups (e.g., "Entrepreneurs Haïtiens," "Business Haïti"). Record in Google Sheets: business name, type, WhatsApp number, source, date added.

2. **Day 1, Hour 2 — Segment the list by business type.** Group: 20 restaurants, 10 pharmacies, 10 boutiques, 10 salons. Customize your opening message slightly for each segment. For restaurants: reference table reservations and menu inquiries. For pharmacies: reference prescription inquiries and product availability. For boutiques: reference product catalog requests. For salons: reference appointment bookings.

3. **Day 1, Hour 3 — Write your cold DM template.** Keep it under 80 words. Example for restaurants: "Bonjou [Pwopriyetè Biznis], mwen rele [Non W]. Mwen bati sistèm WhatsApp pou restoran Pòtoprens ki reponn kliyan otomatikman — menm amidnwi. Ou pa bezwen fè anyen. Mwen ofri yon demo gratis (pa gen obligasyon). Eske ou gen 10 minit semèn sa?" Do NOT send a sales pitch in the first message. Ask only for permission to share more.

4. **Day 1–2 — Send the first 25 messages.** Send no more than 12–15 per day from any single WhatsApp number to avoid being flagged as spam. Space messages at least 3–5 minutes apart. Do not use broadcast — send individually so messages appear personal.

5. **Day 2–3 — Track responses.** Log in your spreadsheet: sent, delivered (two gray ticks), read (two blue ticks), replied. Calculate your read rate and reply rate in real time.

6. **Day 3 — Send the second 25 messages.** Apply learnings from the first batch. If restaurants replied more than pharmacies, send more restaurant-specific messages in this batch. Refine your opening line if read rate is under 40%.

7. **Day 3–4 — Respond to every positive reply within 30 minutes.** For anyone who says "yes, tell me more" or "what is the price?" — immediately send: (a) a 60-second voice note introducing yourself personally, (b) a screenshot of an example WhatsApp automation flow, (c) an invitation to a free live demo via WhatsApp video call.

8. **Day 4–5 — Follow up with non-responders (read but no reply).** Send one follow-up: "Mwen jis voye yon ti mesaj pou wè si ou te gen chans gade sa mwen te voye ou a. Ou ka m di non epi mwen p ap kontakte ou ankò — pa gen pwoblèm ditou." This lowers the social cost of ignoring you and often triggers replies.

9. **Day 6 — Conduct demos with all interested businesses.** For each demo: share your screen (or point camera at phone), walk through an example automated WhatsApp flow for their specific business type. Show them: the greeting message, the FAQ auto-reply, the lead capture sequence, the hours/location card. Ask at the end: "Ou ta enterese kòmanse ak sa a semèn pwochèn?"

10. **Day 7 — Count results and analyze.** Log: messages sent, read rate (%), reply rate (%), demos conducted, trials agreed, deposits collected. Calculate your conversion rate from cold DM to trial.

**Success Threshold:** 5 positive replies, 1 trial closed (deposit received OR verbal commitment with specific start date).
**Failure Threshold:** Fewer than 2 total replies from 50 messages sent.

**If success (5+ replies, 1 trial):**
- This channel works — scale it
- Hire a local part-time outreach assistant at 2,500–3,000 HTG/week to send 50 messages per day
- Build a follow-up sequence: Day 1 intro → Day 3 demo offer → Day 5 social proof → Day 7 deadline
- Create a WhatsApp broadcast list of warm contacts for future offers and updates
- Combine with Experiment 2 (paid ads) — warm outreach + paid traffic is your growth engine

**If failure (< 2 replies):**
- Analyze delivery: were messages delivered (two ticks) or blocked? If blocked, your number may be flagged — use a fresh number or WhatsApp Business account
- Diagnose message quality: was your opening too salesy? Too long? Unclear benefit?
- Try a completely different opener: instead of a pitch, ask for their opinion ("Mwen fè rechèch sou biznis Pòtoprens. Eske mwen ka poze ou yon sèl kesyon?")
- Try a different channel: Facebook Page Messenger instead of WhatsApp, or direct comments on business posts
- Revisit your target list — are these numbers actually active on WhatsApp?

## Moat Strategy

### Strategy 1: Distribution Partnerships

**Description:** Partner with organizations that already serve your target clients — mobile money agents, local banks (BNC, Sogebank), business associations (Chambre de Commerce d'Haïti), and telecom partners (Digicel, Natcom) — to co-sell or refer KoneksAI to their small business client bases.

**First Concrete Action:** This week, identify the 3 most active business WhatsApp groups or Facebook groups in Port-au-Prince (minimum 1,000 members each). Request to post a short introduction from the group admin. Offer a group-exclusive discount of 10% off setup for any member who signs up within 14 days. Track referral source for each signup.

**Expected Payoff:** Access to pre-qualified warm audiences of business owners. A single partnership with a 2,000-member Haitian entrepreneurs group could yield 5–10 inquiries from one post, at zero ad spend. At a 20% close rate, that is 1–2 clients per post per month.

**Time to Implement:** 3–5 days to identify groups and reach admins; 1–2 weeks to negotiate access and post.

---

### Strategy 2: Exclusive Niche Focus

**Description:** Become the undisputed WhatsApp automation specialist for one specific niche — restaurants — before expanding. A generalist gets ignored. A specialist gets referred. When every restaurant owner in Pétion-Ville knows KoneksAI as "the WhatsApp system for restaurants," you become the default choice without competing on price.

**First Concrete Action:** Rewrite all your marketing materials this week to include the phrase "We specialize in WhatsApp automation for Port-au-Prince restaurants." Build one beautifully documented restaurant case study (even from a pilot client or a self-built demo). Create a restaurant-specific lead magnet: a free "WhatsApp Menu Setup Guide for Haitian Restaurants" (1-page PDF) and offer it free to any restaurant owner who DMs you.

**Expected Payoff:** Niche positioning reduces sales cycle by 40–60% because prospects self-identify and pre-qualify. Referral rate within the restaurant community increases because restaurant owners talk to each other. Within 60 days, target 8–10 restaurant clients, then expand to pharmacies with a "restaurant-proven" credential.

**Time to Implement:** 2–3 days to rewrite materials; 1 week to build case study; ongoing.

---

### Strategy 3: Data Capture and Business Intelligence

**Description:** Every client's WhatsApp automation system generates valuable data: which questions customers ask most, which hours drive the most inquiries, what products are most requested. Aggregate this data (anonymized) across your client base to create Port-au-Prince business intelligence reports that only KoneksAI possesses. Use these insights as a retention tool and a marketing asset.

**First Concrete Action:** Starting with your first client, build a simple Google Sheet that logs: most common customer questions answered, peak WhatsApp inquiry hours, most-requested products or services, and lead capture rate by week. After 3 clients, produce a 1-page "Port-au-Prince Small Business WhatsApp Report" and share it publicly on LinkedIn and Facebook. This positions KoneksAI as a market research authority, not just a tools vendor.

**Expected Payoff:** Clients who feel they are getting business intelligence (not just software) churn 50–70% less. The public report generates organic PR and inbound leads from business media. Target: publish first report at month 3, get featured in one Haitian business media outlet (AlterPresse, Le Nouvelliste Business section, or local Facebook news pages).

**Time to Implement:** Immediate (start tracking from Day 1 with first client); first public report at 60–90 days.

---

### Strategy 4: Bundling and Recurring Revenue Lock-In

**Description:** Increase client lifetime value and switching cost by bundling WhatsApp automation with complementary services: monthly performance reporting, quarterly chatflow updates, social media post templates, and WhatsApp promotional campaign management. Price the bundle at a meaningful discount versus buying services separately — but at a higher MRR than automation alone.

**First Concrete Action:** This week, design two pricing tiers. Tier 1 (Esansyèl): $50/month — WhatsApp automation only. Tier 2 (Pwogrè): $100/month — WhatsApp automation + monthly performance report + 2 chatflow updates + 4 WhatsApp promotional campaigns per month. Offer Tier 2 to your first 5 clients at the Tier 1 price ($50) for 90 days as a beta tester reward. After 90 days, convert to full Tier 2 pricing. Show them the data that proves the value before asking them to pay more.

**Expected Payoff:** Average MRR per client increases from $50 to $85 when 40% of clients upgrade to Tier 2. At 20 clients, this means $1,700/month MRR instead of $1,000. Bundled clients churn at half the rate of single-service clients. Time to break even on each client decreases because LTV increases.

**Time to Implement:** 2–3 days to design tiers; offer immediately to first clients.

---

### Strategy 5: Community and Referral Loops

**Description:** Build a private WhatsApp group or Facebook Group for KoneksAI clients — "Biznis Pòtoprens Kap Grandi" — where they can share tips, ask questions, and refer each other. Clients who feel they belong to a community are dramatically less likely to churn and dramatically more likely to refer. Add a structured referral incentive: $10 USD account credit for every new client they refer who pays their first month.

**First Concrete Action:** When you onboard your third client, send all three clients a WhatsApp invitation to join a private group: "Mwen kreye yon gwoup prive pou kliyan KoneksAI yo — yon espas pou pataje eksperyans, kesyon, ak kòman pou grandi biznis ou ak WhatsApp. Ou enterese?" Once 5 clients are in the group, post weekly content: 1 WhatsApp tip, 1 Port-au-Prince business news item, 1 client spotlight. Run a monthly "Best WhatsApp Chatflow of the Month" award with a small prize (1 month free service).

**Expected Payoff:** Community members refer an average of 0.5 new clients per year at zero marketing cost. At 20 clients, this generates 10 additional clients per year from referrals alone — worth $7,500 in setup fees + $6,000+ in annual MRR. Community membership reduces monthly churn by an estimated 30% because clients feel accountable to each other.

**Time to Implement:** Launch group at client 3 (approximately week 4–6); community becomes self-sustaining at 8–10 members.
